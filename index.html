The Core Paradigm

"ON" vs. "IN" Your Business

To scale, we must clearly separate the tasks that drive daily sales from the tasks that drive long-term company growth.

Working IN the Business

Focus: Daily Operations & Execution (Day B Topics)

  • Cooking the food on the line.
  • Ringing up the register and serving guests.
  • Putting out daily fires and managing crises.
  • Result: You keep the doors open today.

Working ON the Business

Focus: Strategy & Scalability (Day A Topics)

  • Writing standard recipes and operations manuals.
  • Reviewing financial reports to increase profit margins.
  • Developing marketing plans and hiring structures.
  • Result: You build a business that runs without you.
Day 1: Overview

Strategy vs. Planning

Strategy is defending your "WHY". Planning is understanding your "HOW".

Strategy (The Vision)

A high-level approach leveraging your unique strengths. It is a long-term vision developed by leadership.

F&B Example: Deciding to become Richmond's premier high-end, plant-based catering company. This shapes your future.

Planning (The Action)

A systematic roadmap for daily targets. It operates in the present to allocate resources.

F&B Example: Creating a timeline to source local vegan vendors, scheduling prep cooks, and designing the winter tasting menu.

Day 2: Goal Setting

SMART Goals vs. OKRs

How do we measure success? Let's break down the two main ways to track our growth.

SMART Goals (Tactical / "IN")

  • Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-Based.
  • Measures one thing at a time for a specific area.
  • Example: "Increase catering revenue by 15% in Q3 by pitching to 5 local offices per week."

OKRs (Aspirational / "ON")

  • Objectives and Key Results.
  • Measures multiple things to track momentum toward a massive vision.
  • A stepping stone from Point A to Point B.

Day 3 & 4: Operations

Systems → Processes → SOPs

Moving from a kitchen that runs on chaos to a business that runs on predictability.

1. System (The WHAT)

A collection of technology, people, and steps working toward a goal.

Ex: Customer Order Fulfillment.

2. Process (The HOW)

The sequence of steps taken from start to finish.

Ex: The journey of a ticket from POS to the Expo window.

3. SOP (The SPECIFICS)

Standard Operating Procedure. A step-by-step guide so simple a new hire can follow it perfectly.

Ex: Plating the signature burger.

Day 5 & 6: Time Management

Controlling the Chaos

"Your success = the quality of your to-do list."

In the F&B industry, everything feels urgent. We must separate the noise from the mission-critical tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix.

  • Manage (Urgent & Important): The line is down during the dinner rush.
  • Focus (Important, Not Urgent): Costing out a new seasonal menu. (Working ON the business).
  • Delegate (Urgent, Not Important): Constant vendor phone calls during prep.
  • Delete (Time-Wasters): Trivial busy-work.

Day 7: Financial Health

Understanding Your Reports

If you don't know your numbers, you don't know your business. Let's define the tools that tell you if you are making money.

P&L (Profit & Loss Statement)

A summary of all money coming in (Revenue) minus all money going out (Expenses) over a specific time. It shows the overall health of the business.

Cash Flow Statement

Cash In minus Cash Out. This is literally how much actual cash you have in the bank today to pay bills, buy equipment, and fix things.

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

The exact direct cost of the raw ingredients and packaging required to produce the food you sell.

Inventory Report

Tracks the quantity of goods you have, the latest unit cost, and calculates the total value sitting on your shelves. Prevents over-ordering and spoilage.

Day 8: Deep Dive Costs

The Holy Grail: Prime Cost

Controlling your Prime Cost is the best and most direct way to increase your net profit.

Prime Cost Formula

Prime Cost = COGS + Total Labor

COGS: Direct cost of raw ingredients.
Total Labor: Wages, salaries, payroll taxes, benefits, and insurance.

Your Prime Cost MUST be ≤ 60% of total sales to survive.

The 30/30/30/10 Rule

An industry standard ratio for healthy cost control:

  • 30% COGS (Food & Bev)
  • 30% Labor Costs
  • 30% Operating Expenses (Rent, POS, utilities)
  • 10% Net Profit (Cash Flow)

Day 9 & 10: Team Building

Leadership vs. Management

The Leader (The Visionary)

  • Motivates people to comprehend and believe in the vision (the WHY).
  • Fosters a trusting kitchen culture.
  • Relies heavily on Soft Skills: Empathy, Communication, Adaptability, Listening.

The Manager (The Expediter)

  • Organizes the systems and ensures day-to-day activities are executed (the HOW).
  • Controls resources, inventory, and scheduling.
  • Relies heavily on Hard Skills: Costing, POS management, Culinary technique.
